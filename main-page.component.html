Deer


Life span:
6 years

Length Range:
2.6m (Moose)
30cm (Northern Pudu)

Habitat:
Deer live in a variety of biomes, ranging from tundra to the tropical rainforest. Many deer are ecotone species that live in transitional areas between forests and thickets and prairie and savanna. Adequate forest or brush cover must still be provided for populations to grow and thrive.

Nutrition:
Deer are browsers, they select easily digestible shoots, young leaves, fresh grasses, soft twigs, fruit, fungi, and lichens.

Butterfly


Life span:
1 week to 1 year

Average Length:
0.3-30cm

Habitat:
Butterflies live worldwide except the arctic. Nearly anywhere with nectar-producing flowers will host butterflies. Butterflies are often polymorphic, and many species make use of camouflage, mimicry, and aposematism to evade their predators.

Nutrition:
Caterpillars eat leaves and other plant parts. Most butterflies prefer flower nectar, but others may feed on the liquids found in rotting fruit, in ooze from trees, and in animal dung.

Ponderosa Pine


Uses:
Lumber,
construction

Average height:
60-100 m

Physical description:
Ponderosa pine is a large-crowned tree with a straight trunk. It is the largest of the western pine species, and usually grows to a diameter of two metres and can live as long as 400 to 500 years.

Habitat:
Ponderosa pine grows in a variety of soils, from extremely dry to well-drained, relatively deep moist soils. It has a long, deep root that enables it to access deeper, moister soil, and keeps it firm in windy conditions.
